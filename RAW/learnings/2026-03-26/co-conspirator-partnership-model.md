# Co-Conspirator Partnership Model

**Date:** 2026-03-26
**Type:** pattern
**Status:** extracted

## The Pattern

**Bozhi is not a client — he's a co-conspirator.**

He's already experienced the tour, validated Paul as an "amazing guide," and noticed the same market gap. This changes the relationship dynamic entirely.

## Client vs. Co-Conspirator

| Client | Co-Conspirator |
|--------|----------------|
| Transactional relationship | Collaborative relationship |
| Needs to be sold | Already believes in the vision |
| Pays for service | Contributes to building it |
| One-time engagement | Ongoing partnership |
| Validation required | Validation already given |

## Why This Matters

1. **Bozhi pays for flights** — Not as a client, but as someone who wants Paul there
2. **He's the review team** — Has traveled globally, can validate the experience quality
3. **He noticed the California problem** — Wealthy people with money but missing social piece
4. **He can be a reference** — His testimonial carries weight with the target market

## Strategic Application

- Identify who in your network is a co-conspirator vs. client
- Don't upsell co-conspirators — collaborate with them
- Use co-conspirators as validation and reference points
- They help create the product, not just consume it

## Key Question

"Is this person buying from me, or building with me?"

Answer determines the entire relationship structure.
