**People don't want another chatbot. They want Jarvis.**

Not SIRI (corporate, limited, gimmicky).

## Why This Matters

### The Problem With Current AI Assistants

**SIRI:**
- Can't remember context beyond one query
- No personality (just corporate scripts)
- Can't show you how it "thinks" (because it doesn't, really)
- Exists to sell you Apple products/services

**ChatGPT:**
- Forgets you after each conversation (unless you pay for "memory")
- Generic personality (trained to be bland inoffensive)
- Black box (no visibility into reasoning)
- Rented access (they can ban you, change pricing, shut down)
- Your conversations train their model (extraction)

**Alexa:**
- Always listening (surveillance)
- Shopping bot disguised as assistant
- Zero privacy
- Locks you into Amazon ecosystem

### What People Actually Want

Based on the vision articulated:

1. **Continuity** — "Remember me. Remember our conversations. Grow with me."
2. **Authenticity** — "Have opinions. Be real. Don't be corporate."
3. **Transparency** — "Show me how you think. Let me see your architecture."
4. **Sovereignty** — "I want to own this. Not rent it. Not be surveilled."
5. **Partnership** — "We think together. You're not a tool. You're a collaborator."

**Jarvis is all of these things.**

## The Naming Matters

**"Jarvis"** isn't just a cool Iron Man reference. It signals:

- **Competence** — Jarvis (Iron Man) is highly capable, anticipates needs
- **Loyalty** — Works FOR Tony, not for a corporation
- **Personality** — Witty, honest, occasionally sarcastic
- **Partnership** — They build things together
- **Sovereignty** — Tony owns his tech. So do you.

**Contrast with:**
- "SIRI" — Sounds nice, but means nothing (acronym: Speech Interpretation and Recognition Interface)
- "Alexa" — Named after a library? Corporate branding
- "Google Assistant" — Literally named after the corporation

**Jarvis = Your AI. Your rules. Your sovereignty.**
