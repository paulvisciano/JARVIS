## 💡 The Decision

**Problem:** "Sovereign" is hard to spell (Paul keeps misspelling it as "sovern")

**Solution:** **Own the misspelling.** Make it the brand.

**Brand:** `sovern.ai`

---

## 🧠 Why This Works

### 1. **Authenticity**
- It's how you actually type it
- No cognitive load ("wait, is it -eig- or -ei-?")
- Matches natural muscle memory

### 2. **Differentiation**
- `sovereign.ai` — generic, probably taken
- `sovern.ai` — unique, memorable, ownable
- Stands out in a sea of "sovereign" projects

### 3. **Humanity**
- Admits imperfection ("yeah, I can't spell it either")
- Relatable — everyone misspells words
- Shows personality, not corporate polish

### 4. **Memorability**
- Unexpected spelling = sticky
- People remember the story ("oh, that's the one without the 'ei'")
- Conversation starter

## 📐 Design Principles

### Visual Identity
- **Logo:** Could play with the missing "ei" (visual gap, stylized text)
- **Colors:** Bold, confident (owning the "mistake" takes guts)

### Voice & Tone
- **Honest:** "Yeah, we dropped the 'ei' on purpose"
- **Confident:** Not apologetic about the spelling
- **Human:** Relatable, not corporate

### User Experience
- **Forgiving:** Search should handle both "sovern" and "sovereign"

- **Consistent:** Use "sovern" everywhere once established
